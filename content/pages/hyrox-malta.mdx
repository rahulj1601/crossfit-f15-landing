# HYROX Malta

HYROX is a fitness race format that pairs eight 1 km runs with eight functional stations: SkiErg, sled push, sled pull, burpee broad jumps, rowing, farmers carry, sandbag lunges, and wall balls. It rewards pacing, grip endurance, and the ability to run after heavy work. F15 Training Centr in Msida runs a dedicated HYROX prep programme built around exactly that demand.

If you are training for a HYROX race in Europe or you want the fitness of someone who could finish one, this is the page for you.

## What HYROX prep at F15 Training Centr looks like

Our HYROX prep block runs twice a week, on Tuesday and Thursday evenings. Each session targets a specific part of the race:

- **Tuesday**: a run-focused session. Intervals on the treadmill or outside, paired with one or two HYROX stations at race pace. Typical work includes 8 x 400 m runs with SkiErg or sled push between efforts.
- **Thursday**: a strength and station endurance session. Heavier sled loads, sandbag work, wall balls, and farmers carry under fatigue. Lower running volume, higher output per station.

Every session is coach-led. The coach sets the standard, watches your movement, and adjusts the load if you are new to a station. Classes stay small so nobody gets lost at the back.

Programming is intermediate by default, with scaling for anyone in their first few weeks. You do not need a HYROX race booked to join. Plenty of our members train HYROX because it builds a useful, transferable fitness: run, push, pull, carry, squat, repeat.

## Why train HYROX in Malta with us

Malta has no dedicated HYROX venue, so most people cobble together their prep from a treadmill, a rower, and hope. That approach misses the specific stations and the specific transitions. At F15 Training Centr we own the kit that matters: sleds with HYROX-spec loading, SkiErgs, Concept2 rowers, wall-ball targets at the right height, sandbags in the race weights, and enough floor space to run a proper sled lane indoors.

The Msida location sits a short drive from Sliema, Gzira, Ta' Xbiex, San Gwann, and Birkirkara. If you live in central or northern Malta, you can be on the gym floor inside 15 minutes. Paola is opening next and will cover the southern harbour area including Fgura, Tarxien, and the Three Cities.

We also run HYROX simulations roughly every six weeks. A full or half race, timed, with the stations set up in order. It is the closest thing to race day you will find on the island, and it tells you exactly what to work on before the next block.

## What a typical Tuesday session looks like

A real session from a recent week:

1. **Warm-up**, 10 minutes. Easy row, leg swings, a-skips, banded shoulder work.
2. **Primer**, 5 minutes. 2 rounds of 200 m run, 10 wall balls, 10 SkiErg calories at an easy pace.
3. **Main piece**, 35 minutes. 5 rounds for time: 400 m run, 20 m sled push at 102 kg, 20 m sled pull at 78 kg, 15 wall balls.
4. **Cool-down**, 10 minutes. Easy walk, hip and calf mobility, breathing reset.

You leave with a clear number on the board, a sense of your race pace, and a coach's note on what to fix next time.
