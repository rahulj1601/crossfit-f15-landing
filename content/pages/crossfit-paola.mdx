CrossFit is coming to Paola. F15 Training Centr is opening a second Malta location in the heart of Paola, bringing the same coach-led CrossFit programming that runs at our Msida gym. If you live in Paola, Tarxien, Fgura, Santa Luċija or Marsa, this will be your nearest CrossFit box.

## What we do

F15 Training Centr runs coach-led group classes built around CrossFit methodology. Every session is programmed by our head coach and delivered by a coach on the floor, not a screen on the wall. Expect a mix of:

- Olympic lifting: snatch, clean, jerk.
- Strength work: back squat, front squat, deadlift, press.
- Conditioning: rowing, ski-erg, assault bike, running.
- Gymnastics: pull-ups, toes-to-bar, handstand work, ring work.
- Classic CrossFit benchmarks: Fran, Cindy, Murph, Grace.

Alongside CrossFit, the Paola site will run HYROX prep sessions built around the eight HYROX stations (sled push, sled pull, burpee broad jump, rowing, farmers carry, sandbag lunges, wall-balls, ski-erg). If you have signed up for HYROX Malta or a European race, this is where you train for it.

New to barbell training? The beginner on-ramp covers the core lifts and gymnastics basics before you join the standard class. You do not need to be fit to start. You need to be willing to show up.

## Why Paola

Paola sits in the south of Malta, ten minutes from Valletta, five minutes from Marsa, and within walking distance of Tarxien and Fgura. The south of the island has been underserved for serious strength and conditioning gyms. Most CrossFit-style options sit in the north or the central belt, which means a drive through traffic at 06:00 or 17:30.

The Paola gym cuts that out. If you work in the Three Cities, live in Żabbar, or commute through Marsa to Valletta, you can train before or after work without crossing the island. Parking is straightforward around Paola square and the surrounding streets, and bus routes from Cospicua, Senglea and Vittoriosa stop nearby.

Our Msida location already trains members from across Malta. Opening in Paola means the south finally has a local option with the same programming, the same coaches, and the same small-class format.

## What a session looks like

Every CrossFit class runs 60 minutes and follows a consistent structure.

1. **Warm-up (10 minutes)**: mobility, activation, and a build into the day's movements.
2. **Skill or strength (15 to 20 minutes)**: a technical lift or gymnastics skill. Could be a heavy triple on the back squat, snatch technique, or strict pull-up progressions.
3. **Workout of the day (12 to 20 minutes)**: the conditioning piece. Scaled for every level in the room.
4. **Cool-down and debrief**: stretch, log your score, talk to your coach.

Class sizes stay small. Your coach knows your name, knows where your squat breaks down, and knows when to push you and when to pull you back. That is the difference between a CrossFit box and a commercial gym floor.
