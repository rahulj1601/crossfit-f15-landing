# CrossFit Msida

F15 Training Centr runs CrossFit classes in Msida seven days a week. The gym is five minutes from the University of Malta, ten minutes from Sliema, and walkable from Gzira and Ta' Xbiex. If you live or work in central Malta, this is the closest coach-led CrossFit box to your door.

We program for adults who want to get stronger, fitter, and more capable. That means barbell work, gymnastics, metabolic conditioning, and enough structure that you can actually see progress week to week. Every class is led by a coach. Every movement is scaled to the person in front of us.

## What we do in Msida

CrossFit at F15 Training Centr is built around constantly varied, functional movement at high intensity, run as a 60-minute class. A typical week covers:

- Olympic lifting: snatch and clean and jerk, with technique blocks before heavy sets.
- Strength: back squat, front squat, deadlift, strict press, bench press.
- Gymnastics: pull-ups, ring work, handstands, toes-to-bar.
- Conditioning: rowing, assault bike, running, wall-balls, thrusters, burpees, sled work.

Classes are capped small so coaches can correct your setup on a deadlift or cue your catch position on a snatch. We know members by name. You will not get lost in the back of a 40-person room.

## Why the Msida location works

Msida sits at the centre of the island. From Valletta it is a 10 minute drive. From Sliema or St Julian's, 10 to 15 minutes outside rush hour. From Birkirkara or Hamrun, under 10 minutes. Parking is available on-site and on the surrounding streets, and several bus routes stop within a short walk.

For students and staff at the University of Malta and Mater Dei, the gym is a short walk or a two minute drive. That matters when you are trying to fit a 06:00 class in before lectures, or a 17:30 session after a shift.

The box itself is kitted for CrossFit and HYROX: rigs, platforms, bumpers, rowers, bikes, ski ergs, sleds, and open floor space for running intervals indoors when the Msida heat or rain makes outdoor work unpleasant.

## What a first session looks like

If you have done CrossFit before, you join a regular class. Tell the coach your background, any injuries, and your recent training load. They will scale the session so it challenges you without breaking you on day one.

If you are new to CrossFit, we start you on the beginner on-ramp. That is a short series of sessions where a coach teaches you the air squat, deadlift, press, and the basic conditioning movements before you join the main class floor. By the end you can follow the daily programming safely and know what the coach means when they call a workout "21-15-9 thrusters and pull-ups".

A session runs roughly like this:

1. Whiteboard brief. The coach walks through the workout, the stimulus, and scaling options.
2. Warm-up. General movement, then specific prep for the lifts or skills in the session.
3. Strength or skill block. Usually 15 to 20 minutes.
4. Metcon. The conditioning piece, anywhere from 4 to 25 minutes.
5. Cool-down and short mobility.

You leave sweaty, coached, and with a number written on the board so you can beat it next time.
