## HYROX Training in Malta: Prep for Your First Race

HYROX has landed in Malta. The format is fixed and repeatable: 1 km run, then a workout station, repeated eight times. Same order, same distances, same standards at every race around the world. If you can handle a steady 5 km and you have a base of strength work, you can finish a HYROX. If you want to race it rather than survive it, you need structured prep.

This guide breaks down the eight stations, the pacing maths, and a Malta-specific training week you can run from Msida. HYROX prep at F15 Training Centr runs Tuesday and Thursday evenings, and the programme is built around the exact movements below.

## The eight HYROX stations, in order

Every HYROX race follows the same sequence. Learn it, because transitions matter almost as much as the work itself.

1. **1000 m SkiErg**
2. **50 m Sled Push** (152 kg men, 102 kg women, including sled)
3. **50 m Sled Pull** (103 kg men, 78 kg women)
4. **80 m Burpee Broad Jumps**
5. **1000 m Rowing**
6. **200 m Farmers Carry** (2x 24 kg men, 2x 16 kg women)
7. **100 m Sandbag Lunges** (20 kg men, 10 kg women)
8. **100 Wall Balls** (6 kg men to a 3 m target, 4 kg women to a 2.75 m target)

A 1 km run separates each station. Total: 8 km of running plus eight stations. First-time finishers usually come in between 1:20 and 1:45. A competitive age-grouper is closer to 1:05, and the elite field breaks the hour.

## Pacing maths for your first race

Most first-timers go out too hard on the first run, then blow up at the sled. The sleds and the wall balls are where races are won or lost, so you pace everything else to protect those two stations.

A sensible target for a 1:30 finish:

- **Runs**: 5:30 to 6:00 per km, consistent across all eight.
- **SkiErg**: 4:30 to 5:00.
- **Sleds**: unbroken if possible, or two sets with short breaks. Keep your chest low on the push, stay patient on the pull.
- **Burpee broad jumps**: steady rhythm, do not sprint the first 20 m.
- **Row**: 2:00 to 2:10 per 500 m split.
- **Farmers carry**: pick up, walk, put down once if needed.
- **Sandbag lunges**: bag high on the shoulders, small steps.
- **Wall balls**: sets of 15 to 20 with 10 to 15 seconds rest.

If you can hit those splits in training, you will hit them on race day. The crowd and the adrenaline add about 3 to 5 percent, not more.

## A sample Malta prep week

This is the week a HYROX-first-timer should be running six to eight weeks out from race day. It assumes you already have a training base of three to four sessions per week.

**Monday - Strength**
Back squat 5x5, Romanian deadlift 4x8, walking lunges 3x20 m, core finisher. The squat and RDL work is non-negotiable for sled and lunge performance.

**Tuesday - HYROX prep (F15 Training Centr)**
Compromised running intervals. Example: 6 rounds of 800 m run, 20 wall balls, 15 m sled push. This teaches your legs to run on fatigue, which is the whole game.

**Wednesday - Easy run or rest**
40 to 50 minutes at conversation pace. The Msida seafront to Ta' Xbiex and back is a flat 5 km out-and-back that works well.

**Thursday - HYROX prep (F15 Training Centr)**
Station-specific volume. Example: 4x 1000 m row at race pace, 4x 50 m sled pull, 4x 100 m farmers carry. Lower heart rate than Tuesday, higher technical focus.

**Friday - Strength**
Deadlift 5x3, strict press 4x6, pull-ups, sandbag carries.

**Saturday - Long session**
Either a full half-HYROX simulation (four stations, four runs) or an 8 to 10 km run with two or three station breaks. Once a month, do a full race simulation.

**Sunday - Rest or mobility**

Five sessions of focused work, one long day, one full rest. That is the rhythm.

## What to train specifically in Malta

A few station-specific notes for anyone preparing in Malta.

**Sled work**: outdoor sled surfaces in Malta are rare. You need a gym with a dedicated sled track. Train the push with a low chest angle and short, driving steps. Train the pull hand-over-hand, not arm-dominant.

**Wall balls**: the height standard catches people out. Train to a taped line at 3 m or 2.75 m, not a random wall. One hundred unbroken wall balls is a realistic goal by race day, but sets of 20 to 25 with short rest will finish faster for most first-timers.

**Running in heat**: if your race is in summer or autumn, practise at least one run per week in the midday heat. Malta summers are punishing, and heat adaptation takes three to four weeks. Hydrate with electrolytes, not just water. The [HYROX official race rules](https://hyrox.com/race-rules/) are worth reading once, especially the no-headphones rule and the rep standards.

**Transitions**: every race includes four to six minutes of total transition time. Practise jogging into the station, starting the first rep within five seconds, and jogging out the moment you finish.
