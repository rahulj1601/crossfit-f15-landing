/ # CrossFit for Beginners in Malta: Your First Month

Most people walking into their first CrossFit class in Malta have the same three worries: the workouts look too hard, everyone else will be fitter, and the barbell movements look complicated. All three are fair. None of them should stop you from starting.

This is a direct walk-through of what the first month of CrossFit looks like at F15 Training Centr in Msida. No hype, no "transformation" promises. Just the structure, the movements, and what to expect from week one to week four.

## Before you start: the on-ramp

The CrossFit on-ramp at F15 Training Centr is built for adults with zero barbell experience. It covers the movements you will see most often in regular classes: air squat, front squat, deadlift, press, push press, kettlebell swing, and the basic gymnastics positions (hollow, plank, ring row).

On-ramp sessions are smaller than regular classes and move slower. The coach explains each movement, shows the common mistakes, and corrects your technique with light loads. A typical on-ramp block runs across three to five sessions depending on your background. If you already lift, it can be shorter. If you have never held a barbell, it takes the full five.

You do not need to finish on-ramp before booking a first class. You can [book your first session](/book) without a membership and the coach will slot you into whatever is appropriate on the day. Some people prefer to start with a one-to-one on-ramp session first. Both routes work.

What to bring:

- Trainers with a flat, firm sole. Running shoes with thick heels are not ideal for lifting.
- A water bottle.
- Clothing you can squat and run in.

That is it. Chalk, bars, rigs, rowers, ski ergs, bikes, and sleds are all at the gym.

## Week one: learning the shapes

Your first week is about positions, not scores. You will not be chasing a time on the whiteboard. You will be learning how to set your back for a deadlift, how to keep your knees tracking over your toes in a squat, and how to finish a press overhead without your ribs flaring out.

A typical class at the Msida gym runs 60 minutes:

- 0 to 10 minutes: general warm-up. Row, bike, or ski, then joint prep.
- 10 to 25 minutes: skill or strength piece. Back squat, push press, or a gymnastics progression.
- 25 to 55 minutes: the workout itself (the "WOD"), with a clear cap and scaled options.
- 55 to 60 minutes: cool-down and clean-up.

In week one the coach will scale everything. A workout written with pull-ups becomes ring rows. A workout written with 60kg front squats becomes an empty bar or a pair of dumbbells. Nobody is watching your weight on the bar. Everyone has been new.

## Week two: the workout actually starts

By the second week the movements stop feeling foreign. You will know where to stand, how to set up your rower, and what "three rounds for time" means. Your heart rate will get high and stay there. This is the week most beginners discover how aerobic CrossFit really is.

A common WOD at this stage might be:

- 4 rounds for time: 400m run, 15 kettlebell swings, 15 air squats.

Simple movements, repeated, at a pace that pushes you. The goal is not to finish first. The goal is to finish with good positions, then recover for the next class.

Expect soreness in week two. Glutes, hamstrings, upper back, forearms. This fades as your tissue adapts, usually by the end of week three. Drink water, eat enough protein, and show up again.

## Week three: loads go up, technique holds

In week three the coach starts adding load to the lifts you have now drilled for ten to fifteen sessions. Back squats might move from an empty bar to 40 or 50kg for a set of five. Deadlifts start to feel like actual deadlifts. Kettlebells get heavier.

This is also the week most people book their membership. You can keep dropping in, but three to four classes a week is the sweet spot for progress, and a membership makes that cheaper per session. If you are training for something specific, like a HYROX race or a hyrox-style event, the coaches will point you toward the HYROX prep sessions that run alongside regular CrossFit classes.

## Week four: a normal member

By week four you are a regular. The coach knows your name, your scaling preferences, and which movements you need to work on. You have a rough idea of your squat, press, and deadlift numbers. You know that 05:30 starts feel rough but you feel sharp by 07:00. You have probably made friends with two or three people who train at the same time each day.

A few things that tend to click in week four:

- Breathing. You stop gasping through workouts and start pacing them.
- Kipping. The foundations of pull-ups and toes-to-bar start to make sense.
- Recovery. You sleep better on training days than rest days.

You are not "doing CrossFit" at this point. You are training. The distinction matters.

## Class times and locations

The Msida gym opens at 05:30 on weekdays. The first class runs at 06:00, with classes continuing through the day to a final evening slot at 20:00. Saturday has a shorter schedule with a longer team-style workout. A second location in Paola is opening and will run the same class format once doors are open.

Class sizes stay small enough that the coach can watch every rep. If you are at the back of the room with bad knee tracking, someone will notice and fix it.
