# CrossFit for Beginners in Malta: What the On-Ramp Looks Like

Most people walking into their first CrossFit class in Malta have the same worry. The workouts on Instagram show people flipping tyres, doing muscle-ups, and snatching a barbell overhead. That is not what your first session looks like. The on-ramp at F15 Training Centr is built for adults who want to learn the basics properly before joining group classes.

This post walks through what beginner CrossFit in Malta actually involves. No hype. Just the movements, the structure, and what to expect across the first four weeks.

## Who the On-Ramp Is For

The on-ramp is for anyone new to CrossFit. That includes people who have never lifted a barbell, runners who want more strength, and gym-goers who are tired of training alone on machines. It also suits people returning after a long break, or anyone coming from a different sport who wants a coached structure.

You do not need to be fit to start. You need to be willing to learn. The on-ramp runs in small groups, usually three to six people, so the coach can correct your setup, your grip, and your bracing before you add load.

If you are already comfortable with squats, deadlifts, and presses, and you have trained in a barbell-based programme before, you can skip the on-ramp and drop straight into a regular [CrossFit class](/). A quick chat with a coach before your first session confirms which route fits.

## The Four-Week Structure

The on-ramp is four weeks. Two sessions per week works for most beginners, though some choose three. Each session is about 60 minutes. Here is how the blocks usually sit across the month.

**Week 1: the three squats and the row.** Air squat, front squat, overhead squat. You learn the foot position, the depth, and how to keep a neutral spine under a light bar. Rowing technique is covered in the same week because it shows up in nearly every conditioning piece. The catch, the drive, the finish, the recovery. Damper setting too.

**Week 2: pressing and pulling.** Strict press, push press, push jerk. Then the deadlift. The deadlift gets its own slot because the setup matters more than the lift itself. Bar over mid-foot, shins close, lats engaged before the pull. Ring rows and banded pull-ups are introduced for the pulling pattern.

**Week 3: the Olympic lifts, scaled.** Power clean from the hang, then from the floor. Power snatch with a PVC pipe and an empty bar. You will not be doing full squat snatches in week 3. You will be learning the shapes and the timing. Wall-balls and kettlebell swings get added to the conditioning.

**Week 4: putting it together.** Short CrossFit workouts using only the movements covered in weeks 1 to 3. Things like an EMOM of 10 deadlifts and 10 wall-balls, or a 12-minute AMRAP of row, press, air squat. You leave with a clear idea of how a class is structured and how to scale any workout to your level.

## What a Session Actually Looks Like

Every on-ramp session follows the same shape, which makes it easier to track your own progress.

- **Warm-up, 10 minutes.** General movement, then specific prep for the day's lifts.
- **Skill and strength, 25 to 30 minutes.** One or two lifts with the coach watching every rep. Load stays light until your technique holds up.
- **Conditioning, 8 to 15 minutes.** A short workout using what you learned that day.
- **Cool-down and questions, 5 minutes.** Stretch, hydrate, ask the coach about anything that felt off.

You will not leave on the floor gasping in week 1. The early sessions are deliberately measured. Soreness is normal, burnout is not the goal.

## Common Beginner Questions

**How sore will I be?** Fairly sore after the first two sessions, then less each week as your body adapts. The worst day is usually day two or three after your first class.

**Do I need CrossFit shoes, knee sleeves, wrist wraps?** No. Flat trainers and comfortable shorts are enough. If you stay with it past the on-ramp, a pair of flat-soled shoes is the first useful purchase. Everything else can wait until you know what you actually need.

**What about injuries or old back problems?** Tell the coach before the session starts. The programme can be scaled around most common issues, including shoulder niggles, knee pain, and lower back history. If something needs a physio rather than a coach, we will say so.

**Can I eat before a morning class?** Something small, 30 to 60 minutes before, works for most people. Coffee and a banana is a common answer. Train a few times and you will learn what your body prefers.
